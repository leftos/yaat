# M12.1 — Efficiency score

**Status:** Stub — not yet planned. Flesh out before implementing.

## Why

M10.7 grades on safety (separation, wake, advisory). A controller can be perfectly safe and still terrible — vectoring everyone 20 miles wide of the optimal path, descending too late, slowing too early. Efficiency is the second leg of the "good ATC" stool and the current Session Report doesn't measure it at all.

## Sketch

Three axes, each producing a per-aircraft score that aggregates into a session-level efficiency grade:

- **Lateral efficiency** — actual track miles flown while on student frequency vs great-circle distance between entry and exit points. Excess vectoring = lower score. Account for procedural turns / pattern entries (don't ding for required PT).
- **Vertical efficiency** — actual altitude profile vs filed / cleared profile. Late descents, step-downs that should have been continuous, climb interruptions — all visible as deviations from a "smooth" profile.
- **Time efficiency** — total time on frequency vs nominal time for the routing. Useful for sequencing pushes (10 arrivals in 15 minutes vs 20 minutes).

Each axis emits per-aircraft data into the Session Report; aggregated as a 0-100 score with a coaching note ("vectored 8 nm wide of the optimal track to sequence behind UAL222 — acceptable for the conflict resolution").

## Open questions

- How does efficiency interact with sequencing? Sometimes the *correct* answer is "vector wide to slot behind a conflicting aircraft." The score has to distinguish "deliberate vectoring for sequencing" from "drift / oversight." Maybe: the coaching layer flags a vector as "efficient given conflict X" if there was a conflict resolution that explains it.
- Vertical efficiency baseline — filed altitude is rarely flown verbatim in real ATC. What's the "ideal"? Maybe just: was the descent monotonic and timed for a 3:1 profile to destination?
- Aggregation — single overall efficiency score, or three separate sub-scores? Probably separate; aggregate is meaningless when one is great and another is terrible.
- Score weighting vs safety — definitely safety dominates. A perfectly efficient session with a separation loss is still a fail.

## Out of scope (probably)

- Real fuel-burn modelling (BADA-grade). Use a proxy: extra-miles × per-type burn-rate constant.
- Comparing against historical sessions for "personal best." Save that for M12.6 (par scoring).
- Per-segment grading (departure / climb / cruise / descent / approach). Keep it whole-track for v1.

## Touches

- `src/Yaat.Sim/Session/` (or wherever M10.7 lives) — new score categories.
- `src/Yaat.Sim/Aircraft/AircraftState.cs` — track aggregation (total miles flown, altitude history). Probably already there from snapshots.

## Pick-up notes

Start with lateral efficiency — it's the most visible and uses data we already have (positions over time, entry/exit points). Vertical efficiency is harder because there's no clean "filed profile" reference. Time efficiency is easiest mathematically but means least without a comparison baseline (M12.6 par scoring fills that gap).
