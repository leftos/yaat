# M10.1.4 — HOO sign-off speech

**Status:** planned (sketch)
**Size:** Small (~2–3 days)
**Maturity:** sketch — needs exploration

## Context

The demo flow described in `README.md` ends with the pilot signing off after a successful HOO: `"Departure on one two five point three five, N123AB, good day."` Today no pilot speech fires when an aircraft is handed off. M10.1.4 adds it.

Originally bundled into M10.1.2 (airborne-spawn check-ins) but split out: HOO sign-off needs ARTCC-config frequency lookup, a facility-shortname mapping ("Tower" / "Departure" / "Center"), and cross-repo plumbing through yaat-server's `TrackCommandHandler.HandleAccept`. Splitting keeps M10.1.2 focused on Yaat.Sim-only airborne check-ins.

## What ships (target outcome)

When a controller HOOs an aircraft and the receiving facility ACCEPTs (the moment ownership transfers in `TrackEngine.HandleAccept`), the simulated pilot says:

- **Next-frequency known** (HOO target resolves to a configured position with a frequency): `"[callsign], [next-facility-name] on [freq], [callsign], so long."`  → e.g., `"American one twenty three, Departure on one two five point three five, American one twenty three, so long."`
- **No next-frequency** (HOO target's facility metadata lacks a frequency, or fallback): `"[callsign], frequency change approved, [callsign], so long."`

When a controller DROPs an aircraft (no further service, no handoff target — common for VFR aircraft leaving Class D after the transition):

- `"[callsign], frequency change approved, [callsign]."` (or simpler "good day")

## Architecture

Two execution paths:

1. **HOO accept path** (cross-repo): `yaat-server/src/Yaat.Server/Simulation/TrackCommandHandler.HandleAccept` calls `TrackEngine.HandleAccept` (in `src/Yaat.Sim/Commands/TrackEngine.cs:118-131`) which transfers ownership at line 125. The cleanest hook: **inside `TrackEngine.HandleAccept` after the ownership transfer**, optionally calling a delegate that yaat-server provides for the frequency lookup. Delegate pattern keeps Yaat.Sim free of ARTCC-config knowledge.
2. **DROP path**: `TrackEngine.HandleDrop` (line 104–116). Simpler — no frequency to look up.

## Needs exploration

Before this becomes active:

- **Frequency-lookup delegate.** What signature? Probably `Func<TrackOwner, double?>` where the result is MHz. yaat-server populates the delegate at SimulationEngine construction; tests stub it. Verify the SimulationEngine constructor / IoC pattern supports adding one more delegate.
- **Facility shortname mapping.** From `TrackOwner.Callsign` (e.g., "OAK_TWR", "SFO_DEP") and `OwnerType` enum, derive the spoken short form. Patterns to handle:
  - `*_TWR` / `*_ATCT` → "Tower"
  - `*_DEP` → "Departure"
  - `*_APP` → "Approach"
  - `*_GND` → "Ground"
  - `*_CTR` / `*_CENTER` → "Center"
  - ERAM positions (different format, no underscore) → ?
- **Frequency formatting.** `125.35` → "one two five point three five" — verify whether `PhraseologyVerbalizer` already has a frequency-words helper (`{tcp}` capture mentioned in M10.1's PilotResponder.cs comment); if not, add one.
- **DROP path triggers.** Confirm DROP fires for the typical "frequency change approved, leaves frequency" case in YAAT scenarios. Today DROP is used for arrival aircraft losing the track at parking; the VFR-overflight-leaves-airspace case may not trigger a DROP, depending on how scenarios script it. May need a new mechanism (or just reuse HOO with a UNICOM-marked target).
- **Test infrastructure.** Existing `HandoffAcceptTests.cs` uses `RoomEngineTestHarness.CreateWithOakData()` plus `TrackHandler.HandleHandoff`/`HandleAccept`. Verify M10.1.4 tests can fit in that pattern.
