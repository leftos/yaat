# M12.6 — Par scoring

**Status:** Stub — not yet planned. Flesh out before implementing.

## Why

Score numbers in isolation are meaningless — "82/100" tells you nothing without a sense of what 82 represents. Real training has the equivalent of a par: a curated good-run benchmark for each scenario. "Par on this scenario is 12 arrivals sequenced in 14:30 with zero vectoring beyond 8 nm." Then your run becomes "14 minutes flat, par minus 30 seconds, two aircraft vectored 9 nm" — concrete, scenario-relative feedback.

## Sketch

- New scenario-author field: `Par` block describing the curated benchmark:
  - Time-to-completion (e.g., 15 minutes from first check-in to last handoff).
  - Efficiency targets (lateral / vertical sub-scores, M12.1).
  - Maximum acceptable findings (zero separation, ≤2 phraseology, etc.).
- For each session, compute the run's metrics against par; show as a "delta from par" panel in the Session Report.
- Authors set par either manually (knowing the scenario) or via a "save current run as par" command on a high-quality run.
- Per-scenario par lives in the scenario JSON. ARTCC-distributed scenarios ship with par; user-modified scenarios can override.

## Open questions

- Does saving-as-par require a verified-clean run, or can it record a sloppy run as par if the author insists? Probably ungated — author owns the file.
- Are par values per-difficulty? Same scenario at "light traffic" and "heavy traffic" would have different par. Probably yes — scope par to scenario-difficulty pair.
- How does par interact with M12.7 (achievements)? "Beat par" is an obvious achievement; "beat par by 20%" tiered. Cross-reference but don't entangle.
- Is "personal best" tracked separately from par? Par is curated, personal best is your own historical max. Useful but more state — defer to v2.

## Out of scope (probably)

- Leaderboards / multi-user comparison. Local only.
- Auto-generated par from a sample of runs.
- Time-to-completion comparisons across different scenarios (apples to oranges).

## Touches

- `src/Yaat.Sim/Data/Vnas/` — scenario JSON schema extension for `Par` block.
- `src/Yaat.Sim/Session/` — par comparison computation.
- Session Report UI — par-delta panel.

## Pick-up notes

Depends heavily on M12.1 (efficiency score) being solid — par is mostly an aggregation of efficiency metrics with a time bound. Don't start M12.6 until M12.1 is stable. The save-as-par flow is the most novel piece; everything else is just storing and diffing numbers.
