# M10.1.2 — Airborne-spawn check-ins (IFR + VFR)

**Status:** shipped 2026-05-02
**Size:** Small (~2–3 days)
**Maturity:** shipped

## Context

A VFR aircraft inbound for full-stop calls Tower / Approach from 5–10 NM out, well before reaching `FinalApproachPhase`. Without an airborne-spawn check-in, a VFR inbound stays silent until on final, which doesn't match real ATC. Same holds for IFR airborne arrivals (handed off mid-cruise) and VFR overflight aircraft transiting the student's airspace.

Originally folded into M10.4's "Pilot expectations + proactive requests" milestone, but the user has called for VFR emphasis up front. M10.1.2 ships airborne-spawn check-ins now. The HOO sign-off that was originally bundled here moves to its own dedicated milestone (see `m10.1.4-hoo-signoff.md`) — sign-off needs ARTCC-config frequency lookup, facility-shortname mapping, and cross-repo plumbing through yaat-server's `TrackCommandHandler.HandleAccept`, all of which deserve their own focused ship.

## What ships

A check-in that fires once per aircraft, post-physics, when the aircraft is first ticked airborne **and** has not yet spoken to ATC (`!HasMadeInitialContact && SoloTrainingMode`). The template branches on `Scenario.StudentPositionType` × `aircraft.FlightPlan.IsVfr` × intent (full-stop / transit / no-destination).

## Templates

Numbers spelled with existing `AtcNumberParser.AltitudeToWords` and `CallsignParser.IcaoToSpoken`; runway formatting via `PhraseologyVerbalizer.SpellRunway`. ATIS information Alpha assumed at all fields.

### IFR airborne

- **Approach (TRACON):** `"[callsign] approach, [spoken] level [altitude], with information Alpha."`
- **Center (ARTCC):** `"[callsign] center, [spoken] flight level [FL]."` (FL180+) or `"[callsign] center, [spoken] level [altitude], with information Alpha."` below
- **Tower (rare — direct-to-tower IFR):** `"[callsign] tower, [spoken] runway [rwy], with information Alpha."` (uses `aircraft.Procedure.DestinationRunway` if known, otherwise drops the runway clause)

### VFR airborne

Intent inference order:

1. `Destination == PrimaryAirportId` → **inbound for landing**
2. `Destination` non-empty and ≠ `PrimaryAirportId` → **request transition**
3. `Destination` blank or null → **VFR transition [heading-direction]bound** (4-point: north/east/south/west, derived from `TrueHeading` at first airborne tick)

The "[direction]bound" form covers negative-flight-following overflight aircraft that have no filed destination — common in real scenarios.

Templates per facility × intent:

- **Tower inbound:** `"[callsign] tower, [spoken] [N] miles [direction] at [altitude], inbound for landing, with information Alpha."`
- **Tower transit:** `"[callsign] tower, [spoken] [N] miles [direction] at [altitude], request transition, with information Alpha."`
- **Tower no-dest:** `"[callsign] tower, [spoken] [N] miles [direction] at [altitude], VFR transition [heading-direction]bound, with information Alpha."`
- **Approach inbound:** `"[callsign] approach, [spoken] [N] miles [direction] at [altitude], request landing, with information Alpha."`
- **Approach transit:** `"[callsign] approach, [spoken] [N] miles [direction] at [altitude], request transition, with information Alpha."`
- **Approach no-dest:** `"[callsign] approach, [spoken] [N] miles [direction] at [altitude], VFR transition [heading-direction]bound, with information Alpha."`
- **Center transit:** `"[callsign] center, [spoken] at [altitude], [N] miles [direction] of [airport], request transition."`
- **Center no-dest:** `"[callsign] center, [spoken] at [altitude], [N] miles [direction] of [airport], VFR transition [heading-direction]bound."`

`[direction]` (relative to airport): 8-point compass quantizer over `BearingTo(aircraft.Position, primaryAirport)` — north / northeast / east / etc.

`[heading-direction]bound` (direction of flight): 4-point compass over `aircraft.TrueHeading` — northbound / eastbound / southbound / westbound.

`[airport]` for Center templates: `Scenario.PrimaryAirportId` lowercased and read as letters (e.g. "K-O-A-K") — Center pilots typically reference the airport directly. Defer that pronunciation polish if it sounds off; readable text is fine for M10.1.2.

### Skipped cases

- **`StudentPositionType == "Ground"`:** no airborne-spawn case (an aircraft spawning airborne wouldn't be talking to Ground).
- **`StudentPositionType` null or unrecognized:** skip — we don't know what facility the pilot is calling.
- **Aircraft already had `HasMadeInitialContact`:** skipped (it took off in-session and already spoke at parking, on the runway, etc.).

## State / data model

No new state fields. Reuses:

- `AircraftState.HasMadeInitialContact` (M10.1.1) — set true after the airborne check-in fires.
- `AircraftState.IsOnGround` — false signals airborne (set to false at takeoff in `TakeoffPhase.cs:125`, or set false directly by `ScenarioLoader` for aircraft preset airborne).
- `SimScenarioState.StudentPositionType` (`SimScenarioState.cs:70`) and `PrimaryAirportId` (line 13).
- `aircraft.FlightPlan.{IsVfr, Destination}`, `aircraft.Position`, `aircraft.TrueHeading`, `aircraft.Altitude`.

## Riskiest part

**Preset SAY commands overlap.** Some scenarios script a preset SAY command for an airborne spawn ("ten miles for the visual" at t=5s). Today's design always fires the auto-check-in at airborne first-tick; a scenario-scripted SAY would then fire shortly after, producing two pilot transmissions back-to-back. For M10.1.2 we accept this overlap — the scenario-supplied SAY will read more specific, the auto-check-in is generic. A follow-up could detect "imminent preset SAY for this aircraft" and suppress the auto-check-in; deferred.

The other risk is `NavigationDatabase` access for primary-airport position lookup — `NavigationDatabase.Instance` is the static singleton, but tests use `SetInstance(db)` (per CLAUDE.md). Hook in `SimulationEngine` should accept a `Func<string, LatLon?>` so tests can stub it.
