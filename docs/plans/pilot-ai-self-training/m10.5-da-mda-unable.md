# M10.5 — DA/MDA contingency + "unable" rejection

**Status:** planned
**Size:** Medium (~1 week)
**Maturity:** drafted — needs revalidation when active

## Context

Two genuine pilot autonomy behaviors that improve training fidelity. The student-controller forgetting to clear a landing should produce a real consequence (the aircraft goes around at minimums); a controller issuing a command the aircraft can't execute should produce a vocalized rejection rather than silent failure.

## What ships

Two genuine pilot autonomy behaviors using a **warn-early-then-miss-at-DA** pattern:

1. **Missed approach with early warning.** When `FinalApproachPhase` descends through `DA + 1000 ft` (or `MDA + 1000 ft` for non-precision) and `aircraft.LandingClearance == null`, push a warning: `"[N123AB] Approaching minimums, no landing clearance, callsign."` to `PendingWarnings` (prominent channel, not notifications). When the aircraft reaches the published DA/MDA without a clearance, autonomously dispatch `GoAround` and push `"[N123AB] Going around, callsign."` to warnings. If a missed-approach procedure is published in the CIFP data, build the missed-approach phase sequence (existing `ApproachClearance.MissedApproachFixes`).

2. **"Unable" responses for impossible commands.** Today, if a command is rejected at dispatch (e.g., `DM 5000` on a parked aircraft), the dispatcher silently fails. Hook the rejection path in `CommandDispatcher` to call `PilotResponder.BuildUnable(cmd, reason) → string` and push to notifications: `"[N123AB] Unable, callsign."` with optional reason if known (`"Unable, we're at the gate, callsign."`).

## Riskiest part

Missed-approach autonomy can hide a student's mistake (they forgot to clear to land — sim quietly goes around). The missed-approach line **must be prominent** in the terminal (warning channel, not notification channel) so the student notices what happened.

- Scenario: parked aircraft, dispatch `DM 5000`. Asserts: dispatch fails AND notifications contain "Unable, ..." line.
