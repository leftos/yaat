# M10.3.6 - Scenario-load training pace controls

**Status:** shipped
**Size:** Small-Medium (~2-3 days)
**Maturity:** drafted

## Context

Many training files are authored with RPO pace control in mind: parked aircraft may be present at load time, but a human RPO can decide when each aircraft calls Ground. Solo-training needs the same pacing control so a student can start with a slower workload and gradually increase difficulty without editing the scenario file.

M10.3.6 adds scenario-load training setup controls for how often aircraft spawned at parking make their initial ready-to-taxi call and how quickly arrival generators add new aircraft. These are not realism rules; they are training-rate controls over real workload sources. The underlying parking call remains based on AIM 4-2-3 initial contact format: facility name, full aircraft identification, surface position, and request. AIM 4-2-2 also supports not jamming a busy frequency and waiting before calling again, which is the training rationale for suppressing some initial calls at lower difficulty.

## What ships

- The existing difficulty overlay becomes a shared **Scenario Setup** modal. It appears only when it has something to ask: multiple scenario difficulties, or solo-training pace controls.
- Add `Parking initial call-up interval` and `Arrival generator rate` sliders to that modal when solo-training pace controls are active and the scenario has the matching workload source: parked startup aircraft for the parking slider, arrival generators for the arrival slider.
- Parking interval range: `Paused` or `10` to `120` seconds, default `20` seconds. Arrival generator range remains `0%` to `100%`, step `5%`, default `100%`.
- The selected values apply to the loaded session/scenario and are remembered as the next defaults. The command-bar gear flyout also exposes the relevant controls live after load so students can recover from workload that is too slow or too fast without reloading.
- Auto-loaded scenarios keep the existing behavior by selecting the hardest difficulty, the default 20-second parking interval, and `100%` arrival generator rate unless a future command-line option explicitly overrides them.

## Implementation shape

- Add synced and replayable session settings named `SoloParkingInitialCallupRatePercent` and `SoloArrivalGeneratorRatePercent`. Defaults are `100`.
- Carry both settings through client preferences, the scenario-load modal view model, client/server session settings DTOs, `SimScenarioState`, snapshots/replay, and server broadcasts.
- `AtParkingPhase` uses the setting before queuing the 5-second ready-to-taxi check-in:
  - `20` seconds is the default spacing between eligible parked aircraft.
  - `10` seconds is the fastest user-facing spacing.
  - `Paused` means no new parked aircraft auto-call until the interval is raised.
  - Waiting aircraft stay unprocessed and retry on later ticks, so every parked aircraft that remains parked can eventually call.
- `SoloArrivalGeneratorRatePercent` is capped at `100`. `100` preserves scenario generator timing, lower values stretch generator intervals proportionally, and `0` suppresses generated arrivals.
- Non-default load-time values and live changes are recorded as `RecordedSettingChange` actions so recording export and replay regenerate the same pacing state.

## Verification

- Client tests: modal appears for difficulty+contextual sliders, difficulty-only, parking-slider-only, arrival-slider-only, and neither; Load/Cancel behavior stays consistent with the current difficulty prompt.
- Preference/session tests: selected pace rates are remembered, sent at scenario load, broadcast to other clients, and replayed.
- Sim tests: the default parking interval spaces calls about 20 seconds apart; `Paused` leaves waiting aircraft unprocessed; faster/slower intervals change future calls; generator percentage behavior remains replayable.
- Manual: load a solo-training scenario with multiple parked aircraft and generators; confirm initial Ground calls and generated arrival pace follow the selected controls without changing the scenario file, then adjust the same controls live from the command-bar gear flyout.
