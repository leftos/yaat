# 7110.65 - Chapter 7: Visual

## Section 7. TRSA

## Section 7. Terminal Radar Service Area (TRSA)-Terminal

#### 7-7-1. APPLICATION

Apply TRSA procedures within the designated TRSA in addition to the basic services described in [Chapter 7](./chap7_section_1.html#KP6r61151Mary), Visual, [Section 6](./chap7_section_6.html#xZF17180Mary), Basic Radar Service to VFR Aircraft- Terminal.

REFERENCE-

FAA Order JO 7110.65, Para [7-2-1](./chap7_section_2.html#tlK384JACK), Visual Separation.

#### 7-7-2. ISSUANCE OF EFC

Inform the pilot when to expect further clearance when VFR aircraft are held either inside or outside the TRSA.

REFERENCE-

FAA Order JO 7110.65, Para [7-2-1](./chap7_section_2.html#tlK384JACK), Visual Separation.

#### 7-7-3. SEPARATION

Separate participating VFR aircraft from IFR aircraft and other participating VFR aircraft by any one of the following:

1. Visual separation, as specified in paragraph [7-2-1](./chap7_section_2.html#tlK384JACK), Visual Separation, paragraph [7-4-2](./chap7_section_4.html#gvJ398JACK), Vectors for Visual Approach, and paragraph [7-6-7](./chap7_section_6.html#bkJ1f4JACK), Sequencing.

   NOTE-

   Issue wake turbulence cautionary advisories in accordance with paragraph [2-1-20](./chap2_section_1.html#drK8cJACK), Wake Turbulence Cautionary Advisories.
2. 500 feet vertical separation.
3. Target resolution, except when ISR is being displayed.

   NOTE-

   Apply the provisions of paragraph [5-5-4](./chap5_section_5.html#PiK190JACK), Minima, subparagraphs g and h, when wake turbulence separation is required.


   REFERENCE-

   FAA Order JO 7110.65, Para [7-2-1](./chap7_section_2.html#tlK384JACK), Visual Separation.

#### 7-7-4. HELICOPTER/POWERED‐LIFT TRAFFIC

Helicopters and powered‐lift aircraft need not be separated from other helicopters or powered‐lift aircraft. Traffic information must be exchanged, as necessary.

REFERENCE-

FAA Order JO 7110.65, Para [7-2-1](./chap7_section_2.html#tlK384JACK), Visual Separation.

#### 7-7-5. ALTITUDE ASSIGNMENTS

1. Altitude information contained in a clearance, instruction, or advisory to VFR aircraft must meet MVA, MSA, or minimum IFR altitude criteria.

   REFERENCE-

   FAA Order JO 7110.65, Para [4-5-2](./chap4_section_5.html#N6M50JACK), Flight Direction.
   FAA Order JO 7110.65, Para [4-5-3](./chap4_section_5.html#tNL3d4JACK), Exceptions.
   FAA Order JO 7110.65, Para [4-5-6](./chap4_section_5.html#HHJ168JACK), Minimum En Route Altitudes.
2. If required, issue altitude assignments, consistent with the provisions of 14 CFR section 91.119.

   REFERENCE-

   14 CFR Section 91.119, Minimum Safe Altitudes: General.
   14 CFR Part 194, Special Federal Aviation Regulation (SFAR) No. 120-Powered‐Lift Subpart C, 194.302 Provisions Under Part 91 Applicable to Powered‐Lift.
3. When necessary to assign an altitude for separation purposes to VFR aircraft contrary to 14 CFR section 91.159, advise the aircraft to resume altitudes appropriate for the direction of flight when the altitude assignment is no longer needed for separation or when leaving the TRSA.

   PHRASEOLOGY-

   RESUME APPROPRIATE VFR ALTITUDES.


   REFERENCE-

   FAA Order JO 7110.65, Para [4-8-11](./chap4_section_8.html#l8I294JACK), Practice Instrument Approaches.
   FAA Order JO 7110.65, Para [5-6-1](./chap5_section_6.html#KRI168JACK), Application.
   FAA Order JO 7110.65, Para [7-2-1](./chap7_section_2.html#tlK384JACK), Visual Separation.

#### 7-7-6. APPROACH INTERVAL

The tower must specify the approach interval.

REFERENCE-

FAA Order JO 7110.65, Para [7-2-1](./chap7_section_2.html#tlK384JACK), Visual Separation.

#### 7-7-7. TRSA DEPARTURE INFORMATION

1. At controlled airports within the TRSA, inform a departing aircraft proposing to operate within the TRSA when to contact departure control and the frequency to use. If the aircraft is properly equipped, ground control or clearance delivery must issue the appropriate beacon code.

   NOTE-

   Departing aircraft are assumed to want TRSA service unless the pilot states, “negative TRSA service,” or makes a similar comment. Pilots are expected to inform the controller of intended destination and/or route of flight and altitude.
2. Provide separation until the aircraft leaves the TRSA.
3. Inform participating VFR aircraft when leaving the TRSA.

   PHRASEOLOGY-

   LEAVING THE (name) TRSA,
   and as appropriate,
   RESUME OWN NAVIGATION, REMAIN THIS FREQUENCY FOR TRAFFIC ADVISORIES, RADAR SERVICE TERMINATED, SQUAWK ONE TWO ZERO ZERO.
4. Aircraft departing satellite controlled airports that will penetrate the TRSA should be provided the same service as those aircraft departing the primary airport. Procedures for handling this situation must be covered in a letter of agreement or facility directives, as appropriate.
5. Procedures for handling aircraft departing uncontrolled satellite airports must be advertised in a facility bulletin and service provided accordingly.

   REFERENCE-

   FAA Order JO 7110.65, Para [7-2-1](./chap7_section_2.html#tlK384JACK), Visual Separation.
