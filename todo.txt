I'd like to introduce the feature of an "approach evaluator", something to score the student on their arrivals. Keep track of arrival rate per airport, and for each approach clearance keep track of intercept angle, distance from FAF at intercept (legal, illegal?), glideslope capture (above? below?), and so on.
                                                                                                                                                  
I'd also like to plan how clearing an aircraft for the "visual approach" would work. When pilots could report the field in sight based on their angle to the field, the weather (e.g. cloud layers between them and the airport), their distance to the field, and have a terminal broadcast once a pilot would have the field in sight so that the RPO can let the student know over voice. Then what would it mean mechanically for a simulated aircraft to execute the visual approach from different places (e.g. downwind, base, straight in, at an angle to the localizer / FAC, etc.)

Also, I'd like to introduce the concept of students telling an aircraft to "expect an approach", and the RPO using a command to put that in. How that can affect things is that generally pilots will only be able to go DCT to fixes they have programmed. An aircraft has their flight plan programmed, but without knowing which approach to expect (unless their last fix only links to one approach really, see the `apps` command in zoa-reference-cli as a reference), they wouldn't have that approach programmed into their flight computer / FMC, so then a DCT would be hard to do. So a DCT for a non-programmed fix would give feedback that the fix isn't programmed, but the RPO could override with DCTF (force direct-to-fix).
